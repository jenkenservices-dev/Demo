DataQ Challenge Guide

Challenge incorrect violations and improve your CSA scores through the FMCSA DataQ system. File challenges within 30 days for best results.

Time-Sensitive Process: File DataQ challenges within 30 days of the inspection for the best chance of success. While you have up to 3 years for violations and 5 years for crashes, early filing significantly improves approval rates.

?
What is DataQ?

DataQ is the FMCSA's electronic system for filing challenges (formally called "Requests for Data Review" or "RDRs") to data in the Motor Carrier Management Information System (MCMIS). This includes roadside inspections, crashes, and investigation results.

Before DataQ, carriers had to contact individual state offices to challenge violations. Now, DataQ automatically routes your challenge to the correct state agency and allows both you and FMCSA to track the status.

How to File a DataQ Challenge

  1. 1

    Create Your Account

    First, you'll need a Login.gov account. Visit the FMCSA DataQ system and click "Register Online". The system will redirect you to Login.gov to create an account if you don't have one.

    Note: Motor carriers should also register through the FMCSA Portal for full access to all inspections associated with their DOT number.
  2. 2

    Gather Documentation

    Collect all supporting evidence before filing:

    • Copy of the inspection report
    • Photos of equipment (if vehicle violations)
    • Driver logs or timesheets (if HOS violations)
    • Court decisions (if citation was dismissed)
    • Mechanic statements or repair receipts
    • Bills of lading or shipping documents
  3. 3

    Select the Correct Challenge Type

    Choose the appropriate category for your challenge:

  4. 4

    Write Your Challenge Statement

    Be clear, professional, and factual. Include:

    • Specific reason why the violation is incorrect
    • Reference to applicable regulations
    • Description of supporting documentation
    • Timeline of events if relevant
    Tip: Avoid emotional language or complaints about the officer. Stick to facts and regulations.
  5. 5

    Submit and Track

    After submitting, you'll receive a confirmation number. Check the status regularly - state agencies typically respond within 10-30 days. Be prepared to provide additional documentation if requested.

Tips for DataQ Success

Proven Strategies from Fleet Managers
  • ‚úì Be Selective: Don't challenge everything. Focus on clear errors or violations with strong documentation.
  • ‚úì Act Fast: File within 30 days while memories are fresh and evidence is available.
  • ‚úì Document Everything: Take photos at roadside, save receipts, get written statements immediately.
  • ‚úì Know the Regulations: Reference specific FMCSR sections to support your argument.
  • ‚úì Track Court Cases: If fighting a citation, use the court decision to support your DataQ.
  • ‚úì Be Professional: State reviewers are more likely to help if you're respectful and factual.
  • ‚úì Follow Up: If no response after 30 days, politely inquire about status.
  • ‚úì Learn from Denials: If denied, understand why to improve future challenges.

Common Successful Challenges

Mis-coded Violations:

Officer selected wrong violation code (e.g., 6-point violation instead of 2-point)

Equipment Working Properly:

Violation for "inoperative" equipment that was actually functioning (with photo proof)

AOBRD/ELD Confusion:

Cited for ELD violation when using grandfathered AOBRD before December 2019

Exemption Not Recognized:

Officer didn't acknowledge valid exemption (agricultural, short-haul, etc.)
